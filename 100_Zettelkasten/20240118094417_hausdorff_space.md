# Hausdorff space

Let $(X, \mathfrak{S})$ be a topological space. This space is a Hausdorff space if for each pair $(a, b)$ of distinct points of $X$, there are neighborhoods $N$ (resp. $M$) of $a$ (resp. $b$) such that $N \cap M = \emptyset$.
